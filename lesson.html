What is Phishing?

Phishing is when bad people pretend to be someone you trust to trick you. They want to steal your passwords, personal information, or money. The word "phishing" sounds like "fishing" because scammers are trying to "catch" you with fake bait!

Phishing can happen through emails, text messages, pop-ups, or even fake websites. The goal is always the same: to trick you into giving away important information or clicking on dangerous links.

How Does Phishing Work?

Scammers use tricks to make you act quickly without thinking. Here's how they do it:

1. They Pretend to Be Someone You Know

Scammers might pretend to be your bank, a popular website like YouTube, or even your school. They use logos and colors that look real to fool you.

2. They Create Urgency

They say things like "ACT NOW!" or "Your account will be closed in 24 hours!" They want you to panic and click without thinking.

3. They Offer Prizes or Gifts

Messages like "You won a free phone!" or "Click here for a gift card!" are almost always scams. If it sounds too good to be true, it probably is!

4. They Use Scary Warnings

They might say "Your computer has a virus!" or "Your password was stolen!" to make you scared and click their dangerous links.

Red Flags: Warning Signs of Phishing

Here are the biggest clues that something might be a phishing scam:

üö© Strange Email Addresses
Real companies use professional email addresses. If an email says it's from Netflix but comes from "netfl1x-support@random-site.com," it's fake!
üö© Spelling and Grammar Mistakes
Real companies check their messages carefully. Lots of typos and weird sentences are warning signs.
üö© Suspicious Links
Before clicking a link, hover your mouse over it. Does the web address look strange or misspelled? Don't click it!
üö© Asking for Personal Information
Real companies will NEVER ask for your password, Social Security number, or credit card info through email or text.
üö© Urgent or Threatening Language
Messages that pressure you to "act now" or threaten to close your account are trying to stop you from thinking clearly.
üö© Unexpected Attachments
Don't open attachments from people you don't know. They could contain viruses!

How to Protect Yourself

üí° TIP: Think before you click! Take a few seconds to ask yourself: "Does this seem real?"

What You Should Do:

  • Check the sender: Look closely at email addresses and website URLs
  • Don't click suspicious links: If you're not sure, don't click it
  • Never share passwords: Not in emails, texts, or pop-ups
  • Ask an adult: If something seems weird, get help from a parent or teacher
  • Use strong passwords: Make them long and different for each account
  • Keep software updated: Updates often include security fixes

What You Should NOT Do:

  • Don't panic when you see urgent messages
  • Don't click on links in unexpected emails or texts
  • Don't download files from people you don't know
  • Don't give out personal information online

Real vs. Fake: Examples

FAKE Message:

"URGENT! Your Roblox account will be deleted in 1 hour! Click here now to save it: roblox-security.fake-site.com"

Red flags: Creates urgency, suspicious link, threatening language

REAL Message:

"Hi! We noticed a new login to your account. If this wasn't you, please visit our official website to change your password."

Why it's safer: No urgent threats, tells you to go to the official website (not a link), calm tone
